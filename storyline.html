Linkin Park Storyline

Linkin Park's story begins in 1996 with high school friends Mike Shinoda, Rob Bourdon, and Brad Delson forming a band called Xero. After adding Joe Hahn, Dave "Phoenix" Farrell, and Mark Wakefield, they released a demo but struggled to find a record deal, leading to Wakefield and Farrell's temporary departure. The band found its iconic sound when they recruited singer Chester Bennington in 1999, changed their name to Hybrid Theory, and then Linkin Park before releasing their breakthrough album Hybrid Theory in 2000. The band experienced massive success until the tragic death of Bennington in 2017. Following a hiatus, the band announced a new era with new vocalist Emily Armstrong and the release of their album From Zero in 2024

Early years (1996–1999)

  • Formation: In 1996, high school friends Mike Shinoda, Rob Bourdon, and Brad Delson formed the band Xero.
  • Expansion: They were later joined by DJ Joe Hahn, bassist Dave "Phoenix" Farrell, and singer Mark Wakefield.
  • Challenges: The band recorded demos but struggled to gain a record deal, leading to frustration.
  • Change in vocalist: After Wakefield left, the band recruited Chester Bennington, whose powerful vocals impressed everyone.
  • Name changes: The band first changed its name to Hybrid Theory in 1999 before becoming Linkin Park in 2000 to avoid confusion with another band.

Rise to fame and later years (2000–2017)

  • Breakthrough album:Their debut album, Hybrid Theory, was released in 2000, catapulting them to worldwide success.
  • Global success:The band achieved massive fame, selling millions of records and completing numerous sold-out tours.
  • Tragedy:Chester Bennington passed away by suicide in 2017, leading to the band taking a hiatus.

New beginning (2017–present)

  • Hiatus:The band members dealt with grief and took a break following Bennington's death.
  • Reformation:The band members eventually began making music together again and collaborated with Emily Armstrong and Colin Brittain.
  • New album and tour:They released their album From Zero in 2024 and embarked on the "FROM ZERO WORLD TOUR," marking a new chapter for the band.
