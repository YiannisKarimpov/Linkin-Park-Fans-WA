Linkin Park Storyline

Linkin Park's story begins in 1996 with high school friends Mike Shinoda, Rob Bourdon, and Brad Delson forming a band called Xero. After adding Joe Hahn, Dave "Phoenix" Farrell, and Mark Wakefield, they released a demo but struggled to find a record deal, leading to Wakefield and Farrell's temporary departure. The band found its iconic sound when they recruited singer Chester Bennington in 1999, changed their name to Hybrid Theory, and then Linkin Park before releasing their breakthrough album Hybrid Theory in 2000. The band experienced massive success until the tragic death of Bennington in 2017. Following a hiatus, the band announced a new era with new vocalist Emily Armstrong and the release of their album From Zero in 2024

Early years (1996–1999)

Formation:

In 1996, high school friends Mike Shinoda, Rob Bourdon, and Brad Delson formed the band Xero.

Expansion:

They were later joined by DJ Joe Hahn, bassist Dave "Phoenix" Farrell, and singer Mark Wakefield.

Challenges:

The band recorded demos but struggled to gain a record deal, leading to frustration.

Change in vocalist:

After Wakefield left, the band recruited Chester Bennington, whose powerful vocals impressed everyone.

Name changes:

The band first changed its name to Hybrid Theory in 1999 before becoming Linkin Park in 2000 to avoid confusion with another band.

Rise to fame and later years (2000–2017)

Breakthrough album:

Their debut album, Hybrid Theory, was released in 2000, catapulting them to worldwide success.

Global success:

The band achieved massive fame, selling millions of records and completing numerous sold-out tours.

Tragedy:

Chester Bennington passed away by suicide in 2017, leading to the band taking a hiatus.

New beginning (2017–present)

Hiatus:

The band members dealt with grief and took a break following Bennington's death.

Reformation:

The band members eventually began making music together again and collaborated with Emily Armstrong and Colin Brittain.

New album and tour:

They released their album From Zero in 2024 and embarked on the "FROM ZERO WORLD TOUR," marking a new chapter for the band.

Mike Shinoda

Michael Kenji Shinoda (/ʃɪˈnoʊdə/ shin-OH-də; born February 11, 1977) is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record producer, and graphic designer. He co-founded the rock band Linkin Park in 1996 and is the band's co-lead vocalist, as well as rhythm guitarist, keyboardist, primary songwriter and producer. Shinoda later created a hip-hop-driven side project, Fort Minor, in 2004. He has also served as a producer for tracks and albums by artists such as Lupe Fiasco, Styles of Beyond, and the X-Ecutioners.

Brad Delson

Delson, alongside Mike Shinoda and Rob Bourdon, founded Xero, the earliest incarnation of Linkin Park, in 1996. The band soon recruited vocalist Mark Wakefield, bassist Dave Farrell, and turntablist Joe Hahn. After Wakefield left Xero, Delson turned to his former boss Jeff Blue from Zomba Music to help find a replacement. Blue introduced the band to Chester Bennington, who successfully auditioned and became the band's full-time vocalist. Blue later became the vice president of A&R for Warner Bros. Records, and helped the band sign a record deal.

Joe Hahn

Hahn began deejaying in high school, and he studied illustration at the Art Center College of Design in Pasadena, California. In college, he met Mike Shinoda and joined his band Xero, which would release its self-titled demo album in 1997. The band later became known as Hybrid Theory in 1999 with the EP release of the same name as the band, then was changed again into Linkin Park during the recording sessions of Hybrid Theory.Hahn and Shinoda guested in the X-Ecutioners' hit single "It's Goin' Down". Hahn also guest starred in Shinoda's Fort Minor's debut album The Rising Tied on the song "Slip Out the Back".

Dave Farrell

Farrell joined Xero, the earliest incarnation of Linkin Park, after meeting Delson at UCLA. He contributed to the band's self-titled demo tape in 1997, but left the project to tour with Tasty Snax. Farrell's void was temporally filled by Delson, Ian Hornbeck and Scott Koziol, who all contributed to Hybrid Theory, Linkin Park's debut album. Farrell returned to Linkin Park in 2000 after a year-long absence. He served as the band's bassist for six of the band's seven studio albums. On the 2002 Reanimation song "Krwlng", the remixed version of "Crawling", Farrell played the violin and cello parts. Along with Delson and Rob Bourdon, he also helped manage the band's business operations.

Emily Armstrong

Armstrong was born in Los Angeles. Her parents were prominent members of the Church of Scientology, and she was raised as a Scientologist. She started to write songs and play guitar when she was 11, and began to sing when she was 15. She dropped out of high school; she said she wanted to be in a rock band and had no interest in pursuing anything else. In an interview with the El Paso Times in 2012, Armstrong said music was the one thing that kept her motivated in life.

Colin Brittain

Colin Cunningham, known professionally as Colin "Doc" Brittain, is an American songwriter, producer, and musician signed to Warner Chappell Music. In September 2024, he joined Linkin Park and has been the band's new drummer since their reunion following the departure of the band's previous drummer Rob Bourdon.
